Affordable one-way flights to Dalaman
Information
The nickname given to the Dalaman area really does say it all. Known as the Turquoise Coast, for its miles of soft sands and deep, azure waters, it's a popular choice for holidaymakers looking for the ultimate Turkish escape. This coastal hotspot offers the perfect mix of cultural and historic sights, white sands and fun attractions and activities.

It boasts stunning scenery and can you completely surrounding yourself with natural beauty. There’s also a wide range of excursions within reach of Dalaman resorts so you can enjoy your holiday, your way, when you choose to visit Dalaman. It is also close enough to neighbouring Bodrum where you can visit the many historical sites southwest Turkey has to offer. Dalaman truly has something for everyone. Easy to get to – just fly straight into Dalaman airport – there is an astonishing array of beaches, lagoons and outdoor activities.
10 facts about Dalaman
1.

In 1906, Alexandra railway station was built by mistake in Dalaman. It still exists but is now used for agriculture.

2.

Sarıgerme Beach is one of the most popular beaches in the Dalaman area and is over 5km.

3.

Marmaris hosts an annual Spring and Maritime festival where activities like sandcastle competitions take place.

4.

Loggerhead turtles are real celebrities in nearby Dalyan, where they come to lay their eggs.

5.

Yassica Island is a collection of five islands just off the coast of Dalaman. These are full of beaches and lagoons and you can swim between them.

6.

The ancient ruins of Ephesus are reachable from Dalaman and you can spend a day there.

7.

Olu Deniz’ Blue Lagoon is often considered to be one of the best beaches in the Dalaman area.

8.

The shops close very late in Dalaman so you don’t need to rush when buying souvenirs.

9.

Olu Deniz hosts quite unmissable air events, where kites of all shapes and sizes are flown.

10.

You can see the famous rock tombs from the sands of Caretta Caretta beach.

Affordable one-way flights to Antalya
Information
A true Turkish delight, Antalya is one of the gems of the Mediterranean. It's become a real favourite with couples and families, and it's easy to see why. As well as the city of Antalya, the coastal resorts in the Antalya area have so much to offer, from amazing beaches and stunning natural scenery, to fantastic fun attractions, to archaeological and historical sights. The city of Antalya itself is a real cultural hotspot, with an amazing wealth of history. Step off the plane and get ready to sample of the very best of Turkey.

Antalya is a Turkish destination that offers much more than your average holiday resort. Rich in history, culture and unbelievable natural scenery, Antalya is well worth the journey. You’ll experience some of the best food along the way as well as very friendly people.
10 facts about Antalya
1.

Antalya is one of Turkey’s most visited cities, up there with neighbours Istanbul and Bodrum.

2.

Antalya mainly manufactures textiles, ships and boats.

3.

The city was founded in the second century BC by Attalus II, who was the King of Pergamum.

4.

The city was first christened Attaleia.

5.

The famous Hadrian Gate, which visitors can enjoy seeing today, was built to commermorate a visit by the great Emperor Hadrian in 130 BC.

6.

Lara beach is easily the best beach in the area and is approximately 12km long.

7.

The Golden Orange film festival is held annually in the autumn.

8.

Antalya is apparently the birth place of Saint Nicholas, who goes by the name Father Christmas.

9.

The length of the shore in Antalya is 640km! That’s a lot of beaches.

10.

Antalya’s old town is surrounded by fortified walls that were restored during the time the Romans and Byzantines ruled.
